# ACI strategic direction: decision-driven aviation intelligence

## Vision

ACI should evolve from a strong analytical foundation with a largely exploration-based experience into a **decision-driven** product that supports route development, market prioritization, and partnership strategy. We will position ACI not as an index alone but as a **supply-side intelligence layer** that complements LASR’s demand signal—giving stakeholders a clearer view of where demand exists, where connectivity constrains it, and what actions could unlock incremental visitor growth. Strategically, ACI is also our path to expand CTDC into **aviation**, a segment where we currently have no subscribers; the capability aligns with how airlines and airports make decisions about routes, capacity, and network planning.

## Evidence basis

Current models and output data already provide a robust analytical base; the gap is product form—translating that depth into repeatable **decisions** rather than open-ended exploration. **Insight** from existing LASR usage (especially US state–level estimates) gives immediate relevance for prioritizing where air-access improvements could move visitor outcomes.

## Risks

- **Adoption risk:** Aviation partners may need co-designed workflows before ACI feels indispensable; mitigate via structured discovery and thin MVPs tied to named decisions.
- **Dependency risk:** MVP value leans on LASR freshness and coverage; unclear or delayed LASR updates could weaken “demand + connectivity” narratives—track data SLAs and fallback messaging.
- **Scope risk:** Simulation and prediction (later horizon) can expand scope quickly; gate funding on validated use cases from the first two quarters.

## Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Next 6 months

- Validate use cases with aviation partners.
- Deliver a lightweight MVP focused on:
  - Priority route opportunities (demand + connectivity gaps).
  - Markets where increased air access unlocks visitor spend.
  - Frequency scenarios with estimated impact.
- Leverage LASR outputs for immediate relevance (especially US state–level estimates).

### Phase 2 — 12–24 months

- Expand into simulation and prediction capabilities.
- Scale to additional stakeholder groups.
- Integrate with Aurora (signal → explanation → recommendation).
